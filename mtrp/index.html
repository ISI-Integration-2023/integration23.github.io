About MTRP

The students and research scholars of Indian Statistical Institute, Kolkata as a part of its annual fest organize the Mathematics Talent Reward Programme (MTRP) in January every year, with the vision of increasing the interest and creating awareness about the subject among school students. One of the major aims of this programme is to identify and reward talent at a young age. This programme also offers the students an opportunity to assess themselves respective to their contemporaries throughout the nation, and brings an opportunity for them to interact with celebrated mathematicians and scientists.


The Mathematics Talent Reward Programme (MTRP) is the perfect platform for all students who are passionate for Mathematics. The pivotal purpose of this programme is to take mathematics beyond standard textbooks by inspiring innovative and non-routine critical thinking among students and improving their conceptual understanding. The Winter Camp, gives an unparalled opportunity to every student to interact firsthand with great academicians and people who hold mathematics, perhaps the closest to their hearts. In the past, MTRP has attracted brilliant minds from all over West Bengal and beyond, who have faced the best mathematical challenges and emerged with flying colours.

Program Itinerary


Overview
Like every year, the Mathematical Talent Reward Programme will have a qualifying examination (Level I), that will be held both online and offline. A student can sit for any mode according to their preference. The selected students in Level I, will be invited to a 2-day Winter Camp at the Indian Statistical Institute, Kolkata. The camp will comprise of guest lectures from the faculty and research scholars of the Institute. Quizzes will be held during the camp (Level II). The students who perform well overall will be felicitated with various awards. Besides this, prizes will be there for all the selected candidates of Level I. (see the Prizes Section for more details).


Exam Pattern
Level I shall consists of a qualifying exam comprising of two sections. Section A would consist of 10 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) and/or Integer Type Questions and Section B would consist of 6 Subjective Questions (single question paper). The total duration of the exam (including both parts) shall be 2 hours and 30 minutes. Evaluation shall be based on a weighted sum of the scores obtained in these two sections. For Level II, there will be 2 quizzes, one on each day of the camp. Each quiz will comprise of 5 questions. The total duration for each quiz shall be 45 minutes (see the Evaluation Section for details).

The syllabus for both the exams consists of (but is not restricted to) well known topics in Number Theory, Combinatorics, Algebra and Geometry. The MTRP Problem Primer gives a thourough training in this regard as to what the students might expect in MTRP as well as prepares them for other Olympiad examinations.


Mode of Examination
This year, the examinations (Level I & Level II) are being conducted via both the Online and the Offline modes. A student can opt for either of these two modes. However, the offline mode will be held in West Bengal alone. The evaluations for both these modes will be done separately and independently (see the Evaluation and Rules & Regulations Section for details).


Categories
The examination will be conducted for two categories of students:
  • Junior Category: Students in or below Class X (10) can apply.
  • Senior Category: Students in Class XI (11) or Class XII (12) can apply.

    Evaluation


    Level I
    • There will be two sections in the qualifying paper: Section A (Objective) and Section B (Subjective).

    • Section A will comprise of 10 questions, some (or all) of which will be Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) and some (or all) will be Integer Type Questions. Of these, three (3) points will be awarded for each correct answer and one (1) point will be deducted for each incorrect answer. No points will be awarded or deducted for any question left unanswered. The maximum, that a student can score in the Objective section is 30 points.

    • Section B will comprise of 6 subjective questions worth 10 points each. Candidates will have to provide a detailed solution to each problem. A solution that is deemed complete by the grader will fetch the contestant full credit. Partial credit will be given in case of an incomplete attempt at a solution depending on the progress made in solving the problem. There will be no negative marking in this section. Innovative solutions will be rewarded with up to 2 bonus points for the corresponding problem. The maximum that a student can score in the Subjective section is 72 points.

    • In order for the Subjective section to be evaluated, the students must score above a certain cut-off (to be determined) in the Objective section.

    • The evaluation for the two modes will be done separately and independently of each other. This means that a student appearing in the Offline mode will only compete with other students in the Offline mode. Same holds for the students in the Online mode. This has been done to ensure that the students in the offline mode are given a fair chance to the camp. Since the evaluations are different, the cut-offs will also be different for the two modes of examination. Eventually, 20 students from the Offline mode and 20 students from the Online mode from each category will be invited to the Winter Camp.

    • Participants are encouraged to submit all rough work as they may lead to the student getting partial credit for the concerned problem(s). There will also be a prize in the camp for the Most Creative Solution, to be determined by the examiners (see the Prizes Section for more details).

    • The final score of a candidate will be the total sum of the Objective and Subjective scores (subject to scoring above the Objective cut-off) and it is based on these composite scores, that the list of candidates selected for the camp in each category and each mode, will be released. Thus, the maximum score a candidate can obtain is 102 points.

    • Candidates are encouraged to answer the examination honestly. Suitable techniques will be employed for detection of cheating. If one or more students are found guilty of cheating, it will lead to immediate disqualification of their candidature. Utmost care shall be taken while grading the answer scripts. Candidates are requested to respect the subjective judgment of the grader(s) regarding creative solutions. No re-evaluation requests shall be entertained.


    Level II
    • The selected students for the Winter Camp will be asked to appear for 2 short quizzes at the end of each day's lectures. Each quiz shall consist of 5 Integer Type Questions, with a duration of 45 minutes. Each question shall carry 5 points and there will be no negative marking.

    • Based on the performance in these quizzes as well as the overall performance in Level I, the top 3 candidates in ach mode and each category, will be awarded prizes (see the Prizes Section for more details).
